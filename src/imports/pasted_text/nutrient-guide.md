Here's every tag in the system, what it is, and what it supports — organised by category.

ANTIOXIDANTS & POLYPHENOLS
Anthocyanins
Water-soluble pigments found in purple, red, and blue foods. Powerful antioxidants that protect cells from oxidative damage. Support brain health, memory, cardiovascular function, and reduce inflammation. Also linked to improved insulin sensitivity and eye health.
Flavonoids
A large family of plant compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. Subcategory of polyphenols. Support heart health, reduce LDL oxidation, improve blood vessel function, and are linked to lower risk of chronic disease. Found across most fruits and vegetables.
Polyphenols
Broad umbrella for plant compounds including flavonoids, tannins, and phenolic acids. General antioxidant and anti-inflammatory action. Support gut microbiome diversity, cardiovascular health, and metabolic function.
Resveratrol
A stilbenoid polyphenol found in grapes and berries. Associated with cardiovascular protection, anti-ageing effects, and anti-inflammatory action. May improve insulin sensitivity.
Punicalagins
Unique to pomegranate. Exceptionally potent antioxidants — among the strongest found in any food. Support heart health, reduce blood pressure, and have anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
Proanthocyanidins
Found in cranberries, aronia, and grape seeds. Prevent bacteria (particularly E. coli) from adhering to urinary tract walls. Also support cardiovascular health and reduce oxidative stress.
Lycopene
Red carotenoid found in tomato and watermelon. Linked to reduced risk of prostate cancer, cardiovascular protection, and skin protection from UV damage. Fat-soluble — absorption increases with dietary fat.
Lutein
Carotenoid found in avocado, kale, and spinach. Concentrates in the retina and macula. Protects against age-related macular degeneration and cataracts. Also supports cognitive function and skin health.
Quercetin
A flavonoid found in apples, onions, and berries. Anti-inflammatory, antihistamine, and antiviral properties. Supports immune function, may reduce allergy symptoms, and protects against oxidative stress.
EGCG (Epigallocatechin gallate)
The most potent catechin in green tea and matcha. Strong antioxidant with anti-inflammatory, metabolic, and neuroprotective effects. Supports fat oxidation, blood sugar regulation, and cardiovascular health.
Phycocyanin
Blue pigment unique to spirulina. Potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compound. Supports immune function and may protect the liver and kidneys.
Chlorophyll
Green pigment in spirulina, chlorella, and wheatgrass. Supports detoxification, blood building (structurally similar to haemoglobin), wound healing, and gut health. May have mild heavy metal chelation properties.
Vanillin
Primary compound in vanilla extract. Mild antioxidant and anti-inflammatory. Primarily a flavour compound but has shown neuroprotective properties in research.
Betalains
Red and yellow pigments in beetroot and Swiss chard. Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory. Support liver detoxification and have been studied for cardiovascular and anti-cancer effects.

VITAMINS
Vitamin A (Carotenoids)
Fat-soluble vitamin obtained from beta-carotene in plants. Essential for vision (especially night vision), immune function, skin cell turnover, and reproductive health. Absorption requires dietary fat.
Beta-Carotene
The most common carotenoid and a precursor to vitamin A. Converted to vitamin A in the body as needed. Supports eye health, immune function, and skin. Also an antioxidant in its own right.
Vitamin C
Water-soluble antioxidant essential for collagen synthesis, immune function, iron absorption (non-heme), wound healing, and skin health. Also regenerates vitamin E. Cannot be stored — needs daily intake.
Vitamin E
Fat-soluble antioxidant that protects cell membranes. Supports skin health, immune function, and cardiovascular health. Works synergistically with vitamin C and selenium.
Vitamin K
Fat-soluble vitamin essential for blood clotting and bone mineralisation. Vitamin K2 specifically directs calcium into bones rather than arteries. Found in leafy greens (K1) and fermented foods (K2).
Vitamin K2
The form of vitamin K found in kefir and fermented foods. Works with vitamin D3 to direct calcium into bones and teeth and away from arteries. Supports cardiovascular and bone health.
Vitamin B6
Water-soluble B vitamin essential for protein metabolism, neurotransmitter synthesis (serotonin, dopamine, GABA), hormone regulation, and immune function. Important for PMS support.
Vitamin B12
Water-soluble vitamin essential for nerve function, red blood cell formation, DNA synthesis, and energy metabolism. Found almost exclusively in animal products. Critical for vegans and vegetarians.
Vitamin B12 (analogue — not bioavailable)
The form found in spirulina. Structurally similar to B12 but not absorbed or used by the human body. Important to flag as it can block uptake of true B12. Not a reliable source.
Vitamin D3
The active, animal-derived form of vitamin D. Regulates calcium and phosphorus absorption, supports immune function, bone health, mood, and may reduce risk of autoimmune conditions. Requires dietary fat for absorption.
Folate (B9)
Water-soluble B vitamin essential for DNA synthesis, cell division, and foetal neural tube development. Supports red blood cell formation and reduces homocysteine levels. Particularly important in pregnancy.
Riboflavin (B2)
B vitamin found in almonds and dairy. Essential for energy metabolism, supports iron absorption, skin health, and acts as an antioxidant cofactor.
Vitamin B3 (Niacin)
Found in peanut butter and animal proteins. Supports energy metabolism, DNA repair, skin health, and cholesterol regulation (at high therapeutic doses).
Thiamine (B1)
Found in macadamia nuts and wholegrains. Essential for carbohydrate metabolism and nerve function. Deficiency linked to fatigue and neurological symptoms.

MINERALS
Calcium
Essential for bone and teeth structure, muscle contraction, nerve transmission, and blood clotting. Absorption is inhibited by oxalates and phytates, and enhanced by vitamin D and vitamin K2.
Iron (Non-heme)
Plant-based iron. Less bioavailable than heme iron from meat. Absorption significantly enhanced by vitamin C and reduced by calcium, tannins, phytates, and oxalates. Essential for red blood cell formation and oxygen transport.
Iron (highly bioavailable)
The chelated form found in iron bisglycinate supplement. Much better absorbed than standard non-heme iron with fewer digestive side effects. Tagged separately to distinguish from food-form iron.
Zinc
Essential mineral for immune function, wound healing, testosterone production, taste and smell, skin health, and protein synthesis. Absorption inhibited by phytates. Found in seeds, nuts, and legumes.
Magnesium
Essential for over 300 enzymatic reactions. Supports muscle and nerve function, sleep quality, bone health, blood sugar regulation, energy production, and stress response. Widely deficient in modern diets.
Potassium
Electrolyte essential for heart rhythm, muscle function, blood pressure regulation, and fluid balance. Works in opposition to sodium. Found in bananas, avocado, coconut water, and leafy greens.
Selenium
Trace mineral essential for thyroid hormone conversion (T4 to T3), antioxidant defence (via glutathione peroxidase), immune function, and fertility.
Manganese
Trace mineral acting as a cofactor for antioxidant enzymes. Supports bone formation, carbohydrate metabolism, and wound healing. Found in berries, oats, and nuts.
Phosphorus
Works with calcium to build and maintain bones and teeth. Also essential for energy production (ATP). Found in dairy, seeds, and nuts.
Copper
Essential for iron metabolism, collagen formation, antioxidant defence, and nerve function. Found in cashews, seeds, and dark chocolate.
Chromium
Trace mineral that enhances insulin sensitivity and supports blood sugar regulation. Found in broccoli and wholegrains.
Iodine
Essential for thyroid hormone synthesis. Thyroid hormones regulate metabolism, energy, body temperature, and cognitive function. Deficiency is the leading cause of preventable cognitive impairment globally.
Silica
Trace mineral found in cucumber and horsetail. Supports collagen synthesis, skin elasticity, hair strength, nail growth, and bone density.

MACRONUTRIENTS & FATS
Protein
Essential macronutrient for muscle repair and growth, enzyme and hormone production, immune function, and satiety. Quality matters — complete proteins contain all essential amino acids.
BCAAs (Branched-Chain Amino Acids)
Leucine, isoleucine, and valine. Directly stimulate muscle protein synthesis. Found in whey protein. Leucine is the key trigger for muscle building.
Glycine
Amino acid dominant in collagen powder. Supports connective tissue repair, sleep quality (as a calming neurotransmitter), gut lining integrity, and liver detoxification.
Proline
Amino acid in collagen. Essential for collagen structure and wound healing. Also supports cartilage and joint health.
Hydroxyproline
Modified amino acid unique to collagen. Stabilises the collagen triple helix structure. Found only in collagen powder and bone broth.
Dietary Fat (MUFA)
Monounsaturated fatty acids found in avocado and olive oil. Support cardiovascular health, reduce LDL cholesterol, improve HDL, and are essential carriers for fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, and K.
Dietary Fat (MCT)
Medium-chain triglycerides found in coconut oil and MCT powder. Rapidly absorbed and converted to ketones for immediate energy. Support cognitive function, fat metabolism, and satiety without being stored as fat as readily as long-chain fats.
Omega-3 (ALA)
Alpha-linolenic acid — the plant form of omega-3. Found in flax, chia, hemp, and walnuts. Must be converted to EPA and DHA in the body, though conversion rate is low (5–10%). Still provides anti-inflammatory benefit and supports cardiovascular health.
Omega-6
Found in hemp seeds and sunflower seeds. Pro-inflammatory in excess but essential in balance. Modern diets are typically too high in omega-6 relative to omega-3.
Lauric Acid
A medium-chain fat in coconut oil. Antimicrobial and antiviral properties. Raises both LDL and HDL cholesterol — effect on cardiovascular health is debated.
Caprylic Acid (C8)
The most ketogenic MCT. Rapidly converted to ketones. Supports cognitive function, gut health (antifungal), and energy.
Capric Acid (C10)
Second most ketogenic MCT. Similar to C8 but slower to convert. Supports energy and antimicrobial defence.
Palmitoleic Acid
Monounsaturated fat found in macadamia nuts. Supports skin health, insulin sensitivity, and cardiovascular function.

FIBRE
Fibre (very low)
Less than 1g per typical serving. Minimal digestive benefit on its own but ingredient still contributes to a fibre-containing blend.
Fibre (low)
Roughly 1–2g per serving. Some digestive and prebiotic benefit. Good supporting ingredient in a fibre-rich blend.
Fibre (medium)
Roughly 2–4g per serving. Meaningful contribution to daily fibre intake. Supports digestion, blood sugar modulation, and gut microbiome.
Fibre (high)
Roughly 4–6g per serving. Significant contribution. Promotes satiety, slows glucose absorption, feeds beneficial gut bacteria.
Fibre (very high)
More than 6g per serving. Notable in seeds (chia, carob) and passion fruit. Major impact on satiety, bowel health, and microbiome diversity.
Soluble Fibre
Dissolves in water to form a gel. Slows digestion, lowers LDL cholesterol, stabilises blood sugar. Found in oats, chia, flax, and psyllium. The type measured as beta-glucan in oats.
Beta-Glucan
Specific soluble fibre found in oats, reishi, and chaga. Clinically proven to reduce LDL cholesterol, improve insulin response, and significantly enhance immune function by activating macrophages and NK cells.
Resistant Starch
Starch that resists digestion in the small intestine and acts as a prebiotic in the colon. Increases in bananas when frozen or when unripe. Feeds beneficial gut bacteria, improves insulin sensitivity, and supports colon health.

BIOAVAILABILITY MODIFIERS
Piperine (bioavailability enhancer)
Active compound in black pepper. Inhibits enzymes that break down curcumin (in turmeric) and other compounds, dramatically increasing their absorption — by up to 2000% for curcumin. Also enhances absorption of selenium, B vitamins, and beta-carotene.
Bromelain
Proteolytic enzyme in fresh pineapple (destroyed by heat and canning). Aids protein digestion, reduces inflammation and post-exercise muscle soreness. Also has mucolytic properties.
Papain
Proteolytic enzyme in papaya. Breaks down protein, supports digestion, reduces bloating, and has anti-inflammatory properties.
Actinidin
Protease enzyme found in kiwi. Significantly improves protein digestion, particularly of dairy and meat proteins. Supports gastric comfort.
Oxalates (inhibitor)
Compounds in spinach, Swiss chard, and beet greens that bind to calcium, iron, and magnesium, preventing their absorption. Effect is reduced by cooking, though smoothie ingredients are raw. Important tag for your scoring engine — pairing spinach with calcium or iron sources should carry a deduction.
Phytates (inhibitor)
Found in seeds, nuts, oats, and legumes. Bind to zinc, iron, calcium, and magnesium, reducing their absorption. Soaking, sprouting, or fermenting reduces phytate content significantly.
Tannins (inhibitor)
Polyphenols found in tea, coffee, pomegranate, and some berries. Bind to iron and reduce its absorption when consumed simultaneously. Timing matters — consuming tannin-rich ingredients with iron-rich ingredients should score a deduction.
Citric Acid
Found in lemon and lime. Enhances non-heme iron absorption (similar mechanism to vitamin C). Also acts as a natural preservative and brightens flavour. Mild chelating effect.

BIOACTIVE & FUNCTIONAL COMPOUNDS
Gingerols
The primary bioactive compounds in fresh ginger. Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antiemetic (reduce nausea). Converted to shogaols when dried or heated, which are more potent anti-inflammatory agents.
Shogaols
Found in dried ginger powder. More potent than gingerols for anti-inflammatory and anticancer effects. Also more bioavailable than gingerols.
Curcumin
The active polyphenol in turmeric. Potent anti-inflammatory — shown in studies to match some anti-inflammatory drugs in effect. Poorly absorbed alone; requires piperine (black pepper) and dietary fat for meaningful bioavailability.
Cinnamaldehyde
Primary bioactive in cinnamon. Reduces blood sugar by mimicking insulin and improving insulin sensitivity. Also has antimicrobial and antifungal properties.
Menthol
Bioactive in mint. Relaxes smooth muscle in the digestive tract, reducing bloating and spasms. Cooling sensation activates TRPM8 receptors. Mild analgesic.
Rosmarinic Acid
Found in mint, rosemary, and tulsi. Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant. Supports cognitive function and may reduce allergy symptoms by inhibiting histamine release.
Eugenol
Primary bioactive in holy basil (tulsi) and cloves. Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and analgesic. Supports cardiovascular health and blood sugar regulation.
Ursolic Acid
Found in tulsi and apple peel. Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant. May stimulate muscle growth, reduce fat accumulation, and support liver health.
L-Theanine
Amino acid found in green tea and matcha. Promotes calm alertness by increasing alpha brain waves. Synergises with caffeine to improve focus without jitteriness. Supports sleep quality.
Melatonin
Found naturally in tart cherries. The primary sleep hormone. Regulates circadian rhythm. Particularly relevant for the evening smoothie timing category in your app.
Tryptophan
Found in pumpkin seeds. The amino acid precursor to serotonin and melatonin. Supports mood, sleep, and anxiety reduction. Requires carbohydrates for efficient brain uptake.
Nitrates
Found in beetroot and celery. Converted to nitric oxide in the body. Dilate blood vessels, lower blood pressure, improve exercise endurance, and support cardiovascular health. Particularly well studied in athletes.
Phthalides
Bioactive compounds unique to celery. Relax smooth muscle in artery walls, reducing blood pressure. Also have mild sedative and anxiety-reducing properties.
Apigenin
Flavonoid found in parsley and celery. Binds to GABA receptors — similar mechanism to benzodiazepines, producing mild calming effects. Also anti-inflammatory and may support oestrogen metabolism.
Sulforaphane
Found in broccoli and kale (activated when chopped or chewed). Potent cancer-preventive compound. Stimulates the body's own antioxidant and detoxification pathways via Nrf2 activation. Anti-inflammatory and neuroprotective.
Hesperidin
Flavonoid in orange and grapefruit. Supports blood vessel integrity, reduces inflammation, and improves circulation. Also has mild blood pressure lowering effects.
Naringenin
Flavonoid in grapefruit. Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory. Inhibits CYP3A4 — the enzyme that metabolises many medications, increasing their blood levels. This is the basis of the grapefruit drug interaction warning.
Actinidin
Already listed above under enzymes — applies here too as it modifies protein bioavailability.
Isothiocyanates
Found in moringa and cruciferous vegetables. Stimulate detoxification enzymes, have anti-inflammatory and potential anticancer effects. Similar mechanism to sulforaphane.
Citrulline
Amino acid found in watermelon. Converted to arginine in the kidneys, which then produces nitric oxide. Supports blood flow, exercise performance, and erectile function.
Cordycepin
Bioactive nucleoside unique to cordyceps mushroom. Supports ATP energy production, oxygen utilisation, and anti-fatigue effects. Studied for athletic performance and respiratory function.
Hericenones & Erinacines
Bioactive compounds unique to lion's mane mushroom. Stimulate Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) synthesis, supporting neuroplasticity, cognitive function, and potential nerve regeneration.
Triterpenoids
Found in reishi and chaga. Anti-inflammatory, liver-protective, and immunomodulating. Also contribute to reishi's mild sedative and stress-reducing effects.
Schisandrins
Bioactive lignans unique to schisandra berry. Support liver detoxification, adaptogenic stress response, cognitive function, and physical endurance.
Rosavins & Salidroside
The two primary active compounds in rhodiola. Work together to reduce mental and physical fatigue, improve stress resilience, support mood, and enhance cognitive performance under stress.
Withanolides
Steroidal lactones unique to ashwagandha. The primary adaptogenic compounds. Reduce cortisol, support thyroid function, improve strength and recovery, reduce anxiety, and support testosterone production.
Eleutherosides
Active compounds in eleuthero (Siberian ginseng). Support physical endurance, immune function, cognitive performance, and stress adaptation. Structurally different from ginsenosides (Panax ginseng).
Ginsenosides
Bioactive saponins unique to Panax ginseng. Support cognitive function, immune modulation, blood sugar regulation, and adaptogenic stress response.
PSK & PSP
Polysaccharopeptides unique to turkey tail mushroom. Among the most studied immune-supporting compounds in functional mushrooms. Enhance NK cell and T-cell activity. PSK is used clinically in Japan alongside cancer treatment.
Betulinic Acid
Found in chaga. Anti-inflammatory and antiviral. Studied for potential anticancer effects. Supports immune function.
Saponins
Found in shatavari, quinoa, and ginseng. Adaptogenic and hormone-supporting in shatavari. Can reduce cholesterol absorption in the gut. Some have mild immune-stimulating effects.
Lignans
Found in flax seeds and sesame. Phytoestrogens that modulate oestrogen activity. Support hormone balance, cardiovascular health, and have antioxidant properties.
Gallic Acid
Found in carob and pomegranate. Antioxidant and antimicrobial. Supports gut health and has anti-inflammatory properties.
Theobromine
Found in cacao. A methylxanthine like caffeine but milder and longer-lasting. Dilates blood vessels, supports cardiovascular health, and provides sustained energy without sharp peaks.

PHYTOESTROGENS & HORMONE MODULATORS
Phytoestrogen
Plant compounds that bind weakly to oestrogen receptors. Can have oestrogenic or anti-oestrogenic effects depending on hormonal context. Found in soy, flax, pomegranate, and shatavari. Relevant for PMS, perimenopause, and fertility support.
Adaptogen
A functional classification rather than a single compound. Adaptogens help the body resist physical and mental stress by normalising physiological processes — they raise what is low and lower what is high. All adaptogens in this system (ashwagandha, rhodiola, maca, shatavari, ginseng, eleuthero, schisandra, tulsi, mushrooms) share this classification.

DIGESTIVE & GUT COMPOUNDS
Probiotics
Live beneficial bacteria (primarily Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium strains). Support gut microbiome diversity, immune function, digestion, mental health (gut-brain axis), and protection against pathogenic bacteria.
Digestive enzyme support
A functional tag covering ginger, papain, bromelain, and actinidin. These ingredients directly support the breakdown of food, reducing bloating and improving nutrient absorption.
Digestive support
Broader tag for ingredients like mint, ginger, and triphala that support overall gut motility, comfort, and function without necessarily containing specific enzymes.
Prebiotic action
Compounds that feed beneficial gut bacteria without being digested themselves. Beta-glucan, resistant starch, inulin, and soluble fibre all have prebiotic properties. Distinct from probiotics (live bacteria).

INHIBITORS (NEGATIVE MODIFIERS)
Oxalates (inhibitor)
Binds calcium, iron, and magnesium. Present in spinach, Swiss chard, and beet greens. Raw is more inhibitory than cooked. Pairing these with calcium or iron sources in the same smoothie should score a deduction in your interaction engine.
Phytates (inhibitor)
Binds zinc, iron, calcium, and magnesium. Present in oats, seeds, nuts, and legumes. Soaking reduces effect but this is not relevant in smoothie context. Should score a deduction when paired with mineral-rich ingredients.
Tannins (inhibitor)
Binds iron primarily. Found in tea, pomegranate, and some berries. Relevant timing consideration — consuming with iron-rich ingredients reduces iron absorption by up to 70%.
High Carb
Not an inhibitor per se but a flag for GI load and blood sugar impact. Relevant to the glycaemic scoring dimension of your app. Ingredients tagged High Carb should push the smoothie's overall GI score upward.
⚠️ Drug interactions (CYP3A4)
Grapefruit-specific flag. Naringenin inhibits CYP3A4, the liver enzyme responsible for metabolising a large proportion of common medications including statins, blood pressure drugs, immunosuppressants, and some antidepressants. Should trigger a user-facing warning in your app.
⚠️ Low nutritional density
Applied to rice milk. Flags that the ingredient adds calories and carbohydrates with minimal micronutrient return, especially compared to other milk alternatives.
Vitamin B12 (analogue — not bioavailable)
Spirulina-specific flag. Structurally mimics B12 and can falsely elevate serum B12 readings while actively blocking uptake of true B12. Critical to flag for vegans who might rely on spirulina as a B12 source.

POTENTIATOR FLAGS
⚡ Potentiates turmeric, fat-soluble nutrients
Black pepper / piperine. Up to 2000% increase in curcumin absorption. Also enhances fat-soluble vitamins and selenium.
Requires Dietary Fat
Flags vitamins A, D, E, K, lycopene, curcumin, and beta-carotene. These compounds cannot be absorbed without fat present in the meal. Pairing them with avocado, nuts, seeds, or oils is a strong positive interaction for your scoring engine.
Requires Black Pepper (bioavailability)
Turmeric-specific. Curcumin alone has very poor bioavailability. Piperine from black pepper is the standard potentiator.
❄️ Freeze-Enhanced
Frozen berries and cherries. Freezing ruptures cell walls, releasing more anthocyanins and making them more bioavailable than their fresh equivalents in some studies.
Heavy metal chelation
Coriander and chlorella. Both have been studied for binding to heavy metals (mercury, lead, arsenic) in the gut and supporting their excretion. Relevant for detox-focused smoothie categories.
NGF stimulation
Lion's mane specific. Nerve Growth Factor supports the survival, maintenance, and regeneration of neurons. Relevant to brain fog, cognitive decline, and nervous system recovery categories.
ATP support
Cordyceps specific. Supports the production of adenosine triphosphate — the primary cellular energy currency. Relevant to energy and post-workout categories.
Blood sugar modulation
Cinnamon and chromium. Distinct from simply being low GI — these ingredients actively improve insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake, providing a functional blood sugar benefit beyond their own carbohydrate content.
Cortisol modulation
Ashwagandha and rhodiola. Both have clinical evidence for reducing elevated cortisol levels. Relevant to burnout, stress, poor sleep, and hormonal imbalance categories.
Liver support
Schisandra and triphala. Support hepatic detoxification pathways. Relevant to general detox, hormone clearance (oestrogen metabolism), and skin health categories.
Immune modulation
Reishi, turkey tail, and elderberry. Distinct from simple immune support — these ingredients regulate immune response up or down depending on context, rather than simply stimulating it.
Antimicrobial
Honey, lauric acid (coconut oil), and caprylic acid (MCT oil). Inhibit growth of bacteria, fungi, and in some cases viruses. Relevant to gut health and immune categories.
